Early Childhood

The Beginnings in Madeira

Cristiano Ronaldo dos Santos Aveiro was born on February 5, 1985, in Funchal, Madeira, Portugal, to a family where soccer was cherished. His father, José Dinis Aveiro, worked as a kit man for a local club, and his mother, Maria Dolores dos Santos Aveiro, was a cook. Ronaldo’s early life in Madeira was shaped by the world of football, instilling in him a deep love for the game.

First Steps in Football

Ronaldo began his formal football journey at Andorinha, where his father was affiliated. By the age of eight, he was already recognized for his dribbling skills. Two years later, he moved to Nacional, one of the top teams in Madeira, before making a significant transfer to Sporting Lisbon, one of Portugal’s biggest clubs.

At Sporting Lisbon, Ronaldo's talent was undeniable. He joined the youth team and quickly rose through the ranks, making his first-team debut by the age of 16. His performance in a match against Manchester United, where he dazzled with his skill and speed, led to his ultimate move to English football, marking the start of an illustrious international career.
